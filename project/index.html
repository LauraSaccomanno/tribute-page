The Beatles Timeline

July 1957

John Lennon meets Paul McCartney at the parish feast at St Peter's Church in Liverpool. After a short time Paul joins John's group, the Quarrymen

1957

A few months later George Harrison joined the group

January 1960

Stuart Sutcliffe, a partner of John at Art Collage, became the bassist of the Quarrymen

August 1960

Pete Best was recruited as a drummer

1960-1962

Period in Hamburg, Germany. The first time the group performed under a contract in the name of "The Beatles" was in Hamburg, on August 17, 1960

September 1962

Ringo Starr replaces Pete Best on drums

1963

Year of the climb to success in which the popularity of the group explodes

March 1963

On 22 March, the first album, Please Please Me, was released

February 1964

First intercontinental trip to the United States

June 1965

The members of the complex would have been awarded the honor of Members of the Order of the British Empire by Queen Elizabeth II

1966-1967

Two years in which, according to many critics, the beatles reach artistic maturity. In these years albums such as Revolver and Sgt. Pepper's Lonely Hearts Club Band were released. The latter is considered by many to be the most important album in the history of rock

1968

The year in which the contrasts within the band begin to be evident. The Beatles album (nicknamed White) is released, a success but the Beatles realized they no longer had the feeling as they used to

January 1969

The Beatles' rooftop concert, the band's last public live performance

1970

The band disbanded. In May, Let It Be, the latest album by The Beatles, is released, finished recording in the studio in January
